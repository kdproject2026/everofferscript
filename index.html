STEP 01 // OPENER
📡 INTRODUCTION
01 Hi, can I speak with [SELLER NAME]?
Warm, natural — don't rush it. Wait for them to respond before moving on.
02 Hey [SELLER NAME], this is John — how are you doing today? [let them respond]
Genuinely wait for an answer. Mirror their energy — if they're short, don't ramble.
03 Good, good — hey, the reason I'm reaching out is I came across your property over on [STREET NAME] in [CITY]. Is that still yours?
Framing it as a question keeps it conversational — not a pitch. Works well even if they're guarded.
04 I wanted to see if you'd had any thoughts about possibly selling — has that ever crossed your mind? [pause — let silence work for you]
"Ever crossed your mind" is softer than "are you selling" — less pressure, more conversation.
STEP 02 // INTEREST CHECK
⚡ SELLER RESPONSE

Select their response to load the correct path:

▶ Oh, perfect timing then. So what we do is put together cash offers — no agents, no fees, no hassle. I just need to grab a couple quick details about the place so I can get the right people to take a look for you. You got about two minutes?
Say "two minutes" not "30 seconds" — sounds more genuine and sets realistic expectations.
▶ Oh, totally understand — I appreciate you being straight with me. Hey, just out of curiosity — do you know anyone in the area who might be looking to sell? We do all-cash deals and can close in as little as two weeks, so it's super easy for the seller.
Don't hang up yet — a referral ask costs nothing. Stay warm, not pushy.
STEP 03 // FACT FIND
🔍 PROPERTY STATUS — SELECT TYPE
🏚 VACANT — QUESTIONS
Q1So the place has been sitting vacant — is that right?
Confirm, don't interrogate. Let them clarify.
Q2Got it — and roughly how long has it been empty?
Longer = more motivated. Take mental note.
Q3Had you figured out what you were going to do with it, or was it more of a "figure it out later" kind of situation?
Light and relatable — keeps it human, not clinical.
Q4Okay, and what's the condition like — is it in decent shape or does it need some love?
"Need some love" is non-judgmental — sellers open up more easily.
Q5And just so I can pass along the right info — did you have a ballpark number in mind, or were you open to hearing what we could do?
If they throw out a number, note it without reacting. Just say "okay, got it."
Q6Perfect. And assuming we could land on a number that works — how soon would you realistically want to move on this?
Timeline = motivation indicator. ASAP is a hot lead.
🔑 RENTED — QUESTIONS
Q1So you've got tenants in there right now — is that right?
Confirm the situation before diving in.
Q2And are they on a month-to-month or locked into a lease?
Month-to-month = more flexible deal. Lease = factor in timing.
Q3Got it. And how's the property overall — in pretty good shape or are there things that need attention?
Q4And what does the inside look like — has it been well kept up?
Tenanted properties can vary a lot. Interior condition matters.
Q5Did you have a number in mind for it, or were you open to hearing what we could put together?
Q6And if things lined up — what kind of timeline were you thinking?
🏠 OWNER OCCUPIED — QUESTIONS
Q1And you're actually living there yourself right now?
Confirm — this changes the whole approach. Be extra respectful of their space.
Q2How long have you been there? [let them share]
This builds rapport. Long-term owners often have equity and emotional ties — be patient.
Q3And the property — is it in pretty good condition overall, or are there some things you've been meaning to get to?
"Meaning to get to" is relatable — removes embarrassment about deferred maintenance.
Q4What's the interior like — has it been updated or is it more original?
Q5Did you have a number in mind, or were you open to seeing what we could come in with?
Q6And if the numbers made sense — is this something you'd want to move on fairly soon, or is timing flexible on your end?
Owner-occupied sellers often need more runway. Don't pressure on timeline.
STEP 04 // PASS OFF
🎯 PASS OFF — CLOSING SEQUENCE
01 Awesome — I've got everything I need. So what happens now is my manager is going to give you a call, probably today or first thing tomorrow, to go over the numbers with you. What's the best number to reach you on?
Get the number naturally — it comes right after telling them what to expect next.
02 Perfect. And hey — totally random question, but do you have any other properties you've been thinking about moving? We're buying a lot in [CITY] right now.
Always ask. Sometimes they have more — or their neighbor does.
03 [SELLER NAME], it was really great talking to you — you'll hear from us soon. You have a good one!
Warm close. Don't drag it out — end confident and friendly.
STEP 05 // OBJECTION HANDLER
🛡 OBJECTION HANDLING — CLICK TO EXPAND
We did a public records search on the property — your name came up as the owner so we just tracked down a contact from there. Nothing weird, just how we find property owners in the area. [then pivot] Anyway, while I've got you — have you ever thought about what you'd take for the place?
→ Normalize it fast and redirect. Most people accept this instantly.
Ha — fair point, I get it. We actually reach out before properties go on the market because sometimes it just makes sense for people to skip all the hassle. But no worries at all. Hey, do you know anyone in the neighborhood who might be thinking about selling? We pay cash and close fast.
→ Lighten it up, don't apologize excessively. Pivot to referral.
Totally fair — are we talking more like a few months out, or more like a year or two down the road? [let them answer]

If months: Got it — well how about I have someone reach back out around that time? I'd hate for you to have to deal with agents and all that when you're ready.

If years: No problem at all — I'll make a note. Is there a good way to reach you when the time's right?
→ Get a timeline, then offer a future follow-up. Every "future" lead is worth tracking.
Honestly, I want to get you as much as we possibly can — but I need to grab a couple details first so the number actually means something. The last thing I want to do is throw out a random figure. Give me two minutes and I'll make sure whoever calls you is ready to put a real number on the table. Sound fair?
→ Don't dodge it — frame why you can't answer yet in a way that benefits them.
Yeah, our main office is out here in California — but we've got a whole team on the ground in [CITY]. The title company, the local reps, all of it is right there. We're pretty active in your market right now, actually.
→ Acknowledge it casually, ground them with local presence.
Totally understand the skepticism — you should be careful. We're Ever Offer, we've been buying properties in [CITY] for a while now — all legit, all through a title company, nothing out of pocket for you. You can look us up. And honestly, you're under zero obligation — my manager would just put together an offer, you look it over, no pressure.
→ Stay calm, don't get defensive. Validate their caution, then make it feel low-risk.
Oh, totally get it — that makes sense. The one thing I'd just say is we're a cash buyer so there's no listing, no open houses, no waiting on financing. Sometimes it's worth having both on the table just to compare. But I hear you — if you're locked in I don't want to complicate things.
→ Don't fight the realtor. Position your offer as a parallel option, not a replacement.
For sure — what's the best email to send it to? [get the email] Perfect. And just so the offer actually reflects your place, do you have two minutes to answer a couple quick questions about the property?
→ Agree immediately, get the email, then use it as a bridge back into the fact find.
STEP 06 // VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS
📬 VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS NO ANSWER

Choose a version based on call attempt number. Keep it short — under 20 seconds.

1ST VOICEMAIL — CASUAL & CURIOUS
VM "Hey [NAME], this is John — I was actually trying to reach you about your property on [STREET]. Nothing urgent, just had a quick question for you. Give me a call back when you get a chance — [YOUR NUMBER]. Thanks!"
"Nothing urgent" and "quick question" reduce defensiveness. Don't mention buying or real estate yet.
2ND VOICEMAIL — SLIGHT REVEAL
VM "Hey [NAME], John again — tried you a bit earlier. I'm reaching out about [STREET ADDRESS]. We're looking at properties in the area and I just wanted to run something by you real quick. [YOUR NUMBER] — hope to hear from you!"
"Run something by you" is vague but intriguing — gives them a reason to call back without triggering a hard sell reflex.
FINAL VOICEMAIL — SOFT CLOSE
VM "Hey [NAME], this'll be the last time I bug you — I promise. This is John, I've been trying to connect about your property on [STREET]. If you've ever thought about selling, even down the road, I'd love to chat. [YOUR NUMBER]. Take care!"
"Last time I bug you" is disarming — people often call back because they feel you respected their time. "Even down the road" removes urgency pressure.
